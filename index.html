Ice Cream Storage & Hardening Room

Ice cream can easily be negatively impacted by temperature change, so it's important to handle it carefully. It is advised to maintain -25 °C room temperature with constant temperature and humidity control. If the cold storage room does not reach -22°C, ice cream will not reach the required hardness and shelf life, which will cause the ice cream to deform and deteriorate.

Sea Food Blast Freezer & Storage Room

Frozen fish products should be stored at temperatures appropriate for the species, type of product and intended time of storage. The recommended storage temperature for all fishery products is -20°C and this temperature has also been adopted throughout Middle East region. Spoilage by bacterial action in any practical sense is completely arrested at this temperature and the rate at which other undesirable changes proceed is greatly reduced.

Meat & Poultry Blast Freezer & Storage Room

If meat is not going to be used within a few days after purchase, it should be frozen as soon as possible to preserve optimal quality. Harmful bacteria begin to develop in raw meat from the moment an animal is slaughtered, making storage an incredibly time sensitive process. If you want or need to prolong the life of your meat for as long as possible, it’s crucial that you follow the correct safe storage procedures. Freezing is the most common method of meat preservation. The ideal temperature for the storage of fresh meat is -18°C to -22°C.

Fruits & Vegetables Storage Room

Most fruits and vegetables have a very limited life after harvest if held at ambient harvesting temperatures. Post-harvest cooling rapidly removes field heat, allowing longer storage periods. The low temperatures inside cold storage units halt the growth of these pathogenic fungi, ensuring that spoilage of fruits and vegetables is kept to a minimum. This is why our cold storage units have a varied temperature range for both freezing and chilling options.

Dry Fruits Storage Room

Dried fruit is the fruit from which the majority of the original water content has been removed. Most dry fruits have a limited life after processing if held at normal temperatures. Proper storage in low temperature can reduce respiratory activity and degradation by enzymes and stops production of natural ripening agent i.e. ethylene. In order to maintain vitamin C, storage temperature can be 2°C to 10°C for a storage time of more than a year with a H of 80% to 95%.
